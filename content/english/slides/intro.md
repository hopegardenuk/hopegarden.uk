<h6 class="breadcrumb">Ideas — <u>Forest garden</u> — Wildlife garden — Community Assembly</h6>

???

* **Always** keep the ground covered
* Annuals, use a mulch. Forest garden, use ground cover plants
* When establishing, use bark or wood chip, plus temporary ground cover
* Wood chip mulch [does not rob nitrogen](https://puyallup.wsu.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/403/2015/03/wood-chips.pdf) (PDF)!
* I prefer bark but wood chip more available
* Best temporary ground cover in West Wales, [White Mustard](https://pfaf.org/user/plant.aspx?LatinName=Sinapis+alba)—buy bulk

# Guidelines

![Dangly purple flowers of comfrey, daft white dog in background](https://res.cloudinary.com/growdigital/image/upload/w_420,h_241/v1543959826/comfrey-flower-29851000577.jpg)

1. [Windbreak](/windbreak/)
2. [Tree spacing](/spacing/)
4. [Ground cover](/ground-cover/)
5. [Nutrients](/nutrients/) 👈

<h6 class="breadcrumb">Ideas — <u>Forest garden</u> — Wildlife garden — Community Assembly</h6>

???

* Calculate **[nutrient budget](https://www.natureworks.org.uk/nutrients/)**, Creating a Forest Garden Ch.6
* Have soil test for nutrients & pH, local farmers co-op
* Make sure to have enough “system plants” providing nutrients
* NPK — Nitrogen, Phosphorous, Potassium
* Nitrogen: alder, pea family, Sea Buckthorn, Autumn Olive
* Potassium: [comfrey](/comfrey/)
* Phosphorous: not really provided by plants
* ['Bocking 14'](https://www.organiccatalogue.com/products/comfrey-bocking-14-plants_mh6390), sterile, bred for nutrients
* [Dwarf Comfrey](https://www.rhs.org.uk/Plants/75444/Symphytum-ibericum/Details), good thick ground cover
